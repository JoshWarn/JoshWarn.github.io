Plotted above are the segments for all primes below a hundred million.

Right-hand Truncatable Primes in Multiple Bases

This calculates RHT Primes (eg. 3, 37, 3733, 37337 in b-10) in a branching fashion. Due to the large Big O for bases,
I implemented fully asynchronous multithreading, redundant task claiming, and various other optimizations.

Currently, the largest base that has been fully computed is b-87 with a digit-length of 53 - being approximatly 6.2*10^102. Due to the large size of the primes, determining primality can be slow even when using a probabilistic test such as Baillie-PSW. To negate this, I have written other methods concerning individual digits - though they are only effective when the prime canidates are sufficiently large.
